description: Hedda Morrison Photographs of China is a collection of 8,579 photographs
  and related materials held by Harvard-Yenching Library, Harvard University, documenting
  China from 1933 to 1946. Hedda Morrison (1908-1991), a German-born photographer
  based in Beijing, captured daily life, architecture, landscapes, religious sites,
  cultural practices, and social conditions across the Republican era and early wartime
  period. The collection spans urban and rural scenes across multiple regions and
  is accessible through Harvard's CURIOSity Digital Exhibits platform, with materials
  organized by creator, date, genre, and subject.
