description: The Dispersed Chinese Art Digitization Project (DCADP), launched in 2019
  by the Center for the Art of East Asia (CAEA) at the University of Chicago, seeks
  to document, preserve, and digitally restore Chinese artworks taken from their historical
  sites of origin and dispersed to collections worldwide during the late nineteenth
  and early twentieth centuries. The project places digitized objects back into their
  original historical and spatial contexts through collaboration between CAEA and
  Xi'an Jiaotong University, with participation from numerous museums and cultural
  sites internationally. The digital collection is accessible through the CAEA library
  interface at the University of Chicago.
